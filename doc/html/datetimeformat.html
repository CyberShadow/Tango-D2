Date and Time Formatting

DateTime format strings determine how dates and times are represented as strings. Standard DateTime format strings consist of a format specifier denoting a commonly used string representation of a date and time. Custom format strings can be used to define patterns specific to an application.

DateTime format strings are accepted by the toString method of the DateTime type, and by the interpolated formatting feature of the Formatter type.

Standard DateTime Format Specifiers

The following table shows and describes the standard DateTime format specifiers.

Format Specifier Name Description
d

 Short date

 Defined by the shortDatePattern property.

For example, "dd/MM/yyyy".
D

 Long date

 Defined by the longDatePattern property.

For example, "dd MMMM yyyy".
f

 Full date/time (short time)

 Combines the long date and short time patterns.
F

 Full date/time (long time)

 Defined by the fullDatePattern property.

For example, "dd MMMM yyyy HH:mm:ss".
g

 General date/time (short time)

 Combines the short date and short time patterns.
G

 General date/time (long time)

 Combines the short date and long time patterns.
m or M

 Month day

 Defined by the monthDayPattern property.

For example, "MMMM dd".
r or R

 RFC1123

 Defined by the rfc1123Pattern property.

The format is "ddd, dd MMM yyyy HH':'mm':'ss 'GMT'", regardless of the culture.
s

 Sortable date/time; ISO 8601 standard

 Defined by the sortableDateTimePattern property.

The format is "yyyy'-'MM'-'dd'T'HH':'mm':'ss", regardless of the culture.
t

 Short time

 Defined by the shortTimePattern property.

For example, "HH:mm".
T

 Long time

 Defined by the longTimePattern property.

For example, "HH:mm:ss".
u

 Universal sortable date/time

 Defined by the universalSortableDateTimePattern property.

The format is "yyyy'-'MM'-'dd HH':'mm':'ss'Z'", regardless of the culture.
U

 Universal sortable date/time

 Defined by the fullDateTimePattern property.

The pattern is the same as the full date/time pattern, with the DateTime value converted to UTC time.
y or Y

 Year month

 Defined by the yearMonthTimePattern property.

For example, "yyyy MMMM".

Custom DateTime Format Specifiers

A custom DateTime format string consists of any number of custom DateTime format specifiers.

The following table shows and describes the custom DateTime format specifiers.

Format Specifier Description
d

 The day of the month as a number between 1 and 31.
dd

 The day of the month as a number between 01 and 31.
ddd

 The abbreviated name of the day of the week. Defined in the abbreviatedDayNames property.
dddd

 The full name of the day of the week. Defined in the dayNames property.
h

 The hour as a number between 1 and 12.
hh

 The hour as a number between 01 and 12.
H

 The hour as a number between 0 and 23.
HH

 The hour as a number between 00 and 23.
m

 The minute as a number between 0 and 59.
mm

 The minute as a number between 00 and 59.
M

 The month as a number between 1 and 12.
MM

 The month as a number between 01 and 12.
MMM

 The abbreviated name of the month. Defined in the abbreviatedMonthNames property.
MMMM

 The full name of the month. Defined in the monthNames property.
s

 The seconds as a number between 0 and 59.
ss

 The seconds as a number between 00 and 59.
t

 The first letter of the AM/PM designator. Defined in the amDesignator or pmDesignator property.
tt

 The AM/PM designator. Defined in the amDesignator or pmDesignator property.
y or yy

 The year as a two-digit number.
yyy

 The year as a three-digit number.
yyyy

 The year as a four-digit number.
z

 The time zone offset from UTC, in hours, ranging from -12 to +13. A single-digit offset is formatted without a leading zero.
zz

 The time zone offset from UTC, in hours, ranging from -12 to +13. A single-digit offset is formatted with a leading zero.
zzz

 The time zone offset from UTC, in minutes and hours, ranging from -12 to +13. A single-digit offset is formatted with a leading zero.
:

 The time separator. Define in the timeSeparator property.
/

 The date separator. Define in the dateSeparator property.
"

 Displays the literal value of any string between two quotation marks.
'

 Displays the literal value of any string between two quotation marks.
